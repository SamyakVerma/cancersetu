## Oral Cancer in India

| Stat | Figure | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| New oral cancer cases/year | **77,000** | GLOBOCAN 2021 |
| Deaths/year | **52,000** | GLOBOCAN 2021 |
| Cases from rural areas | **74.9%** | ICMR NCRP |
| Diagnosed at Stage 3 or 4 | **80%** | ICMR NCRP |
| Share of all cancers in India | **30%** | ICMR NCRP |

## Why This Matters

- Late-stage diagnosis (Stage 3/4) has a **5-year survival rate of <30%**, vs. **>80%** if caught at Stage 1.
- Rural areas lack oncologists — the patient-to-specialist ratio in rural India is **1:500,000+**.
- ASHA workers (900,000+ across India) are the last-mile healthcare touchpoint but lack AI-powered diagnostic tools.
